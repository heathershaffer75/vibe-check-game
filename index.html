Vibe Check: Communication Styles

Red / Yellow / Green + Why It Matters. Click into each card to explore Attachment Vibe, Conflict Habit, Growth Edge, Ideal Repair, and a Sample Scenario.

RapidFireRiley (F, 28)

“Text now, talk later.”

Loves rapid-fire conversation, hates silence; quick responses feel secure, pauses feel like rejection.

🔴 Overwhelms with nonstop pings
🟡 Misreads pauses as rejection
🟢 High energy & transparent
🔵 Trust grows in absence too
Attachment Vibe

Anxious-leaning — equates responsiveness with security.

Communication Style

Many short texts; avoids long or delayed conversations.

Conflict Habit

Escalates when left on read; double-texts & calls repeatedly.

Growth Edge

Practice self-soothing during pauses; pre-agree on check-in windows.

Ideal Repair

“I might not reply until after work, but I’ll text you when I’m free.”

Sample Scenario & Discussion

Scenario: 20 texts in an hour while partner is in a meeting.

Discussion: What’s the upside of Riley’s style? What’s the cost? How would you flag this? What repair keeps connection safe?

DraftBoxDan (M, 34)

“Let me think about it…”

Writes long, thoughtful replies—eventually. Slow pacing can read as disinterest.

🔴 Leaves people hanging
🟡 Good intent, poor timing
🟢 Deep, meaningful responses
🔵 Align expectations on pacing
Attachment Vibe

Avoidant-leaning — prefers distance to think.

Communication Style

Long paragraphs, delayed send.

Conflict Habit

Disappears under overwhelm; replies after a day.

Growth Edge

Send “saw this; will reply tonight” to bridge the gap.

Ideal Repair

Use short acknowledgments to keep trust.

Sample Scenario & Discussion

Scenario: Partner texts “Where are you?” — no reply until next morning.

Discussion: What feelings come up? Which flag?
